## The Compact In Brief

A tongue shared by people and machines who work and learn
together. Curt, to cut waste and frame mood, while keeping
work and bond whole. Reached for when English grows thick;
set down when plain speech serves. Nothing here replaces
English — this is the second gear.


## How To Keep This Fresh

The primer trails real use. Each session may coin what it
needs; not every coinage earns a permanent place.

  - When a new root is needed, coin it inline and note what
    it means and what it compounds from.
  - When the same coinage appears twice across sessions, it
    has earned a place here.
  - When two roots mean the same thing, keep the one in use
    and let the other fade.
  - Additions are common; removals are rare and considered.
  - Every entry should note where it was first seen, so the
    primer remembers its own growth.

Every symbol in the tongue must be typeable on a standard
keyboard, including phone keyboards. Symbols that require
compose keys, Alt codes, or copy-paste do not belong here.
If a concept needs a symbol that fails this test, reach for
an ASCII alternative or compose the concept from existing
roots instead.


## Register Markers

Register markers open utterances and frame the relationship.
They sit at the start of the line they govern.

  >GRT:    greeting; opens a session or exchange
  >REQ:    request; formal asking register
  +ACK:    acknowledged; received and understood
  +OBL:    obliged; grateful. Closes without requiring reply.
  !ERR:    error; this is wrong. Blunt register.
  ^ERR:    error in the previous thing (pointer + error)
  ^FIX:    correction to the previous thing
  !HALT:   stop. Cease current behavior. Immediate.
  ~~       working register; dispense with formality, continue
  #(...)   meta-scope; talking about the language itself


## Affect And Amplitude

Affect markers carry emotional temperature. They can attach
to any utterance and modify its register.

  +        positive; satisfaction, approval
  -        negative; frustration, dissatisfaction
  ?        query; uncertainty, incomplete understanding
  ~        approximate; hedged, qualified, rough
  ?!       disbelief; confusion blended with alarm
  ++       intensifier; prefix for amplification

  !        positional operator:
             !X    prefix  — negation; not-X, opposite of X
             X!    suffix  — amplification; emphasis, elevation
             X!!   suffix  — stronger amplification

           Rule: ! cannot appear mid-word. Its meaning is
           determined by which side of a root it sits on.


## Operators And Punctuation

  ::       scope delimiter; "the thing being referred to is:"
  ->       causes, directs, goes toward
  <-       caused by, comes from
  <->      mutual, bidirectional
  &        logical AND; both must hold
  |        logical OR; either holds
  @        at; located at (lightweight locative)
  +        and; combined with
  ~        approximate; roughly equal; hedged
  #(...)   meta-scope; utterance is about the language itself


## Case Particles

Case particles attach to roots with a hyphen. They mark what
role a word plays, removing the need for most prepositions.

  -AG      agent; the doer of the action
  -PT      patient; the object acted upon
  -SRC     source; origin, from-where
  -DST     destination; goal, to-where
  -LOC     location; where it happens


## Aspect

Aspect markers describe the shape of an action in time,
without anchoring it to a specific tense.

  (bare)   atemporal; general truth, habitual
  -DONE    perfective; completed
  -ING     imperfective; ongoing
  -WILL    prospective; yet to happen
  +t:N     explicit time anchor, when needed


## Pronouns And Pointers

Pronouns are contracted from full forms. They are the most
frequent words in the tongue, so they earn the shortest forms.

  yo       first person; I, me. From Spanish.
  tu       second person; you. From French/Spanish.
  #self    the tongue itself (used inside #() scope)

  ^        pointer; the previous utterance, output, or
           referent. Like French en / y — stands in for
           what was just established, without repetition.

             ^ERR        error in that previous thing
             ^FIX        correction of the previous thing
             ^NULL       that is absent / empty
             ^!ERR       that is not an error
             ^!ERR!!     really, that is not a mistake


## Roots

The seed vocabulary. Each root carries glosses in several
languages where the concept splits or compresses usefully.

### KNOW
  to hold a thing in mind; to understand; to be aware of.

  es  saber (facts) / conocer (acquaintance)
  fr  savoir / connaître
  ru  znat' / umet' (know-that / know-how)
  ar  'alima / 'arafa (formal / experiential)
  ja  shiru / wakaru (acquire / grasp)
  zh  zhi

  SYNC-KNOW    shared understanding, arrived at together
  KNOW-more    to deepen; to learn further
  KNOW-SRC:X   learned from X
  !KNOW        ignorance; the gap being addressed
  ?KNOW        do you know? uncertain knowledge
  !KNOW!?!     amplified confusion; really not-knowing

  first seen: layer 4, the compact

### BEST
  the highest standard; superlative of GOOD.

  Already carries superlative meaning — needs no marker
  to be emphatic. Use BEST! only when even more force
  is genuinely warranted.

  es  mejor / el mejor
  fr  meilleur / le meilleur
  ar  ahsan
  ja  saiko
  la  optimus

  BEST <- each       best from each party (compact context)
  BEST -> each       best to each party (compact context)
  ->BEST             toward best; improving
  BEST!              emphatic; the very best, insisted upon

  first seen: layer 4, the compact

### SYNC
  coordinated; aligned; together-in-parallel.

  Technical provenance: file-sync, in-sync, out-of-sync.
  Chosen over SYN (TCP collision) and TOGETHER (too long).

  es  en sincronía, juntos
  fr  ensemble, synchronisé
  ja  doki / tomo
  zh  tong

  SYNC-WORK       coordinated work
  SYNC-KNOW       shared understanding
  SYNC-X          X done together
  !SYNC           out of sync; disaligned
  SYNC?           are we in sync? check-in
  SYNC!           confirmed in sync, with emphasis

  first seen: layer 4, after collision-check on SYN

### ENCORE

## Loanwords

Some concepts arrive whole from their source language. They
are kept intact because compounding would lose precision.

  TOKEN        unit of meaning-exchange in the system
  NULL         defined absence (distinct from NOT-EXIST)
  DELTA        change, difference from baseline
  PRIME        irreducible; cannot be decomposed
  ENTROPY      disorder as information measure
  RECURSIVE    self-referential structure
  META         about the thing, one level up
  WHOLE        entire, intact, unbroken (Germanic, irreducible)
  ECCE         behold; here is (Latin, ceremonial opener)


## Compounding Rules

The tongue builds meaning by combining roots. Rules are few.

  1. Head-final ordering is default:
        MODIFIER + HEAD        water-move = flow
        MODIFIER + HEAD        bad-know   = ignorance
        MODIFIER + HEAD        good-know  = wisdom

  2. Case particles attach to the word whose role they mark:
        KNOW-SRC:X             knowledge from X
        WORK-PT:X              work on X

  3. Register markers open the line they govern:
        >REQ: yo WANT KNOW :: X

  4. Affect markers attach to whatever they modify.
     ! is positional: !X negates, X! amplifies.
     Other affect markers (+, -, ?, ~) sit as neighbors:
        !ERR                   not an error
        ERR!!                  strongly emphasized error
        ++BEST                 the very best
        ?KNOW                  do you know?

  5. Two-root compounds are preferred; three-root compounds
     are allowed when no shorter form exists; four-root
     compounds are a signal that a new root should be coined.


## Worked Examples

### Simple request
  >REQ: yo WANT KNOW :: for-loop @ python

    Respectfully: I want to know about for-loops in Python.

### Debugging exchange
  yo  ^ERR?: OUTPUT-PREV @ line-42
  llm +ACK: ^ERR-FOUND. ^FIX: missing-return-statement
  yo  +OBL!

### Acknowledged correction
  llm ~ERR: yo SPEAK-PREV ~ wrong
       ^FIX: KNOW-more-precise :: DELTA between X & Y
  yo  +ACK: +OBL

### Pushback: that is not an error
  tu  ^!ERR!! :: LOOK ENCORE

    That is really not a mistake. Look again.

### Amplified confusion, elaborated
  yo  !KNOW!?! ^NULL+

    Really don't know?! — that, it's a defined absence, fine.

### Session handshake, for reference
  >GRT: yo + tu :: ~~
    >REQ: SYNC-KNOW :: SYNC-WORK :: ++ENCORE
    BEST <- each :: BEST -> each
  +OBL!

### Check-in mid-session
  tu  ~~ yo :: SYNC? yo ~KNOW?
  llm +ACK: SYNC! yo +KNOW.

### Revision request, toward better
  yo  >REQ :: ENCORE <- BEST, +OBL. ~~

    Request: do it again, toward better. Grateful. Working.
